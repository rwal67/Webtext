Snap fingers

One of these days, I'll go and sit by the docks.

I'll watch the boats go by.

I'll go where they're dyed like saffron, mustard, and thyme.

They'll lend you so many strange questions

and leave before you answer.

Like all those sorry things you had:

the friends

the trends

the fears

and all that you held dear,

they fade,

a little at a time.

Swallowed by a setting sun; they faintly disappear.

Yes, I'll go to the docks, I'll ask to leave with them

but they'll be too far to hear.
